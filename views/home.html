Expressive
Voters list on their ballot any candidates for which they would want their vote cast, in preferential order. Given all ballots, if a voter's candidate can win, the vote is cast. Otherwise, the next candidate listed is used and the process starts again.
Inheriently Unconflicted
An approach in which it is inherently impossible to "split the vote" or "waste your vote".
Idealist and Pragmatic
This creats a system in which voters: may vote their conscience, may not have to just choose between the lesser of two evils, may be idealist and pragmatic.
Non-Binary
This would spell the end of the two party monolopoly. Similiar candidates (or parties) are not punished. Voters may identify with parties and individuals outside of a binary party system. This also means parties could effectively carry more than one candidate through the process.
